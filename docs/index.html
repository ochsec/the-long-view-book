The world is getting better. The evidence is overwhelming.

Every day, the headlines tell us the world is in crisis. But the data tells a different story. Global poverty is at its lowest point in human history. Child mortality has dropped by two-thirds in a single generation. Life expectancy has doubled in the last century. Violence is declining. Education is spreading. Technology is accelerating.

The Long View is a rational, evidence-based case for optimism — not blind hope, not denial of real problems, but a clear-eyed look at what the data actually says about human progress.

What's inside

Spanning 11 chapters across four parts — Evidence, Mind, Work, and Vision — The Long View walks through the data on human progress, the psychological biases that prevent us from seeing it, the systems and institutions that drive improvement, and what we can build next.

It draws on decades of research from Hans Rosling, Steven Pinker, Max Roser, and dozens of other scholars who have dedicated their careers to measuring what actually changes in the world. Every claim is sourced. Every statistic is verifiable.
